Tirui (Steve) Cao

Final-Year PhD Student, Tsinghua University

Hi there, I’m Tirui Cao, and some friends call me Steve. I am currently a final-year PhD student in the Department of Industrial Engineering at Tsinghua University, advised by Professor Lei Zhao. During my PhD, I visited the MIT Sloan School of Management as a visiting student, hosted by Professor Alexandre Jacquillat.

Broadly speaking, I develop mathematical models and scalable algorithms for large-scale combinatorial optimization problems, with a focus on transportation science and logistics (TSL). My research is motivated by emerging applications and practical challenges faced by leading industry companies such as JD Logistics, SF Express, and Alibaba Group. Methodologically, I focus in particular on exact methods—such as Dantzig–Wolfe decomposition, column generation, and branch-price-and-cut—for integer optimization, as well as on approximate dynamic programming (now more widely known as reinforcement learning) for sequential decision-making under uncertainty.

I am also strongly interested in applying my expertise in optimization and analytics to real-world problems. I have participated in industry collaboration projects with Huawei, COSCO Shipping, and OOCL. Additionally, I interned at Alibaba Group and Meituan, which broadened my methodological toolkit to include causal inference and machine learning.
