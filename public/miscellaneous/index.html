More about Me

  1. I am a huge foodie, especially when it comes to Japanese cuisine and sushi 🍣.

    • According to my photo archive, I had more than 550 pieces of sushi in 2024 alone.
    • I hosted three Japanese culinary events in Beijing and curated the menus for each of them.
    • I interviewed a female kaiseki chef—which is still quite rare in this field—and wrote an article published by a media outlet.
    • Like many food lovers, I once thought my passion for food would naturally lead to cooking. After quite a number of failed attempts, however, I have had to admit that I am sadly not a good cook 😞.

  2. I am also a wine and spirits enthusiast, with a particular fondness for Japanese sake, Champagne, and cocktails. I am always on the lookout for memorable bottles, hidden gems, and beautifully crafted drinks. One of my favorite bottles is Dom Ruinart Rosé 1996.

    • Read my tasting note on Ruinart Rosé 1996

      Very, very old—so old that it had all but lost its sparkle. It took me quite a while just to ease the cork out. Served thoroughly chilled, it poured into the glass with a gorgeous apricot hue. A soft veil of condensation gathered on the bowl, as alluring as a beauty fresh from the bath.

      On the palate, it was graceful and slender, with only the faintest trace of mousse lingering in delicate strands—an almost teasing touch, like the subtle artistry of a master tea ceremony. As the wine slowly warmed, it unfurled completely: cream 🧁, butter 🧈, cake 🍰, cocoa 🍫, coffee ☕️, caramel 🍬, fully ripe pear 🍐, and just a tiny hint 🤏 of lemon zest, as if I had dived headfirst into a jar filled with candied lemon peel.

      Vincent asked me which I preferred: this, or Krug Rosé. My answer was that they were simply too different to compare. One was the beauty of finesse; the other, the beauty of grandeur. One seduced in whispers; the other burned with passion. Which would I choose? Both, without hesitation.

      Note: Originally written in Chinese and translated into English with ChatGPT.

      —June, 2024
